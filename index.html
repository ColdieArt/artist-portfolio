About Coldie

A Pioneer of NFT Digital Art

Coldie is one of the most respected and collected artist in the history of NFT digital art. Active in the crypto art space since 2017, he is widely recognized as one of the earliest and most influential crypto artists, and the first artist to tokenize stereoscopic 3D art and photography on the blockchain.

Born in California in 1982 and holding a BFA from Cal Poly Pomona, Coldie's artistic journey began at age 4 on a Macintosh using MacPaint. His formative years in Los Angeles -- working on covers at LA Weekly and developing a deep obsession with production, display, pricing, and collectability -- laid the foundation for a career that would help define an entirely new art movement.

He tokenized his first SuperRare piece on July 24, 2018 -- "Decentral Eyes -- Vitalik Buterin -- Variant 01" -- launching the iconic Decentral Eyes portrait series: a collection of stereoscopic 3D portraits of influential figures in blockchain and culture, each composed of 10+ collaged images rendered in his proprietary stereoscopic 3D technique.

With career NFT sales exceeding $7 million, Coldie is one of the highest-earning crypto artists. His collaboration with Snoop Dogg, "Decentral Eyes Dogg," sold for 188.8 ETH (~$754,300) on SuperRare -- one of the most significant NFT art sales in history.

Why Coldie Is a Pioneer of NFT Art

◈

First Stereoscopic 3D NFTs

Coldie is the first artist to tokenize stereoscopic 3D art and photography on the blockchain, creating an entirely new visual category for digital art. His proprietary technique uses red/cyan anaglyph 3D, requiring glasses to experience the full depth effect -- making the viewer fully immersed in the artwork.

◎

Invented the Timed Auction Format

Coldie pioneered the reserve-price-triggered 24-hour countdown auction format, now known as the "Coldie Method." SuperRare officially adopted and codified this into their smart contracts in December 2020 -- even embedding a Coldie reference on-chain. Foundation and other platforms later adopted the same mechanic.

◆

10% Resale Royalty Standard

In 2019, Coldie was part of the group of early crypto artists who drafted and sent a declaration to all platforms to adopt a 10% resale royalty standard. This became the industry norm and has financially benefited thousands of digital artists worldwide.

●

Recognized by Pak as a "Builder"

In April 2021, Coldie was acknowledged as one of only 30 recipients of "The Builder" NFT as part of Pak's "The Fungible" collection at Sotheby's, recognizing artists who paved the way for the entire NFT art movement.

Career Timeline

1982 - Early Years

Born in California

Began digital art at age 4 on grandparents' Macintosh using MacPaint. Discovered a passion for 3D through View-Masters and Magic Eye posters. In grade school, learned collage using "Art Explosion" clip art collections.

2006 - Los Angeles

Career Beginnings

First design work at IKEA and LA Weekly at age 24 in Los Angeles. Worked on editorial covers, then transitioned to freelance for creative freedom. Earned a BFA from Cal Poly Pomona.

2009 - 2010

Coachella Official 3D Photographer

Served as official 3D photographer at Coachella, capturing Red Hot Chili Peppers, The XX, Foals, and more using a custom twin-lens DSLR camera. Began extensive stereoscopic concert photography project spanning through 2018.

2017

Entered the Crypto Art Space

One of the first artists to begin exploring blockchain technology for art. Started experimenting with tokenizing stereoscopic 3D art -- becoming the first to do so on the blockchain.

July 2018

First SuperRare Piece

Minted "Decentral Eyes -- Vitalik Buterin -- Variant 01" on SuperRare, launching the iconic Decentral Eyes portrait series. Also collaborated with XCOPY on "DystoPunk 3D" -- one of the earliest NFT collaborations.

2019

Royalty Standard & Creative Expansion

Co-drafted the 10% resale royalty declaration sent to all crypto art platforms. Created GANdinsky 3D -- his first generative art experiment. In his first 20 months, tokenized only 89 pieces (all 1/1 editions), prioritizing scarcity.

December 2020

"Coldie Method" Adopted by SuperRare

SuperRare officially codified Coldie's reserve-price timed auction format into their smart contracts, embedding a Coldie reference on-chain. This format was subsequently adopted by Foundation and other NFT platforms.

2021

Breakthrough Year

Multiple landmark events: Pak's "The Builder" recognition at Sotheby's. Proof of Work Genesis sold for $100,313 at Bonhams. Bauhaus collaboration "Undead" on Nifty Gateway. Snoop Dogg's "Decentral Eyes Dogg" sold for 188.8 ETH ($754,300). Featured at Christie's "The Gateway" during Miami Art Week.

2022

Gazelli Art House Solo Exhibition

"Front Row 3D: Stereoscopic Concert Photography" solo exhibition at Gazelli Art House, London. A manifestation of Coldie's dream to display his stereoscopic concert photography as fine art in full-color VR and lenticular prints.

Present

Continuing the Vision

Continuing to create, collect, and curate. Active collector and advocate for artist rights. Career sales exceeding $7M across SuperRare, Nifty Gateway, Async Art, Bonhams, and other platforms. The Decentral Eyes series remains one of the most iconic collections in crypto art history.

In His Own Words

"I think what happens is that a lot of people sidestep art -- they can just scroll, even in a physical circumstance such as in an art museum. But when you make art that can only be seen through 3D glasses, you are fully immersed until you take the glasses off."
-- Coldie, on stereoscopic art | Anna Seaman Interview
"When I started in 2017, all I wanted to do was pave the way for other artists who came after me. The journey that followed was a manifestation of 'going for it' with calculated experimentation."
-- Coldie, on pioneering the NFT art movement
"Decentral Eyes was born out of the goal to contextualize the faces that were shaping the crypto space in real time... Vitalik made perfect sense to be the first featured because his technology was powering the very token the art is minted on."
-- Coldie, on the Decentral Eyes series
"I am always using my design to pay homage to the past but to keep it current. And the main thing is that it has to have a point. I want to reach people."
-- Coldie, on artistic philosophy
"The opportunity to collaborate with Bauhaus was an easy decision as I look at them as pioneers and influential music creators. This parallels how I have found myself pioneering in the crypto art and NFT space."
-- Coldie, on the Bauhaus collaboration | Rolling Stone
"When I examine art that I am considering collecting, my first gut check is the 'ok, so what?' test. Does this artwork strike me in a special way? Is there a message that I pull from it?"
-- Coldie, on collecting art | Kate Vass Galerie Interview
